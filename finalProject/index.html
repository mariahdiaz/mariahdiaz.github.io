Our Ever-Growing World




By Mariah Diaz

Motivation

This project is intended to increase understanding of population growth throughout the world. Specifically, how population size varies throughout the world, trends in population over time, and a country’s population density in relation to its geographic size.

Choropleth Map

The first visualization I have implemented is a Choropleth Map to visualize population distribution across the world. I have chosen this visualization because it gives a clear picture of the world as a whole and helps a viewer visually connect a country to its precise location and current population. I have implemented a hover tooltip that gives specific information for each country to make the overall experience even clearer. A sequential color scale has been chosen because when viewing a quantitative value, such as population that only has positive values, it makes the most sense.

Multi-Series Line Chart

I picked a line chart for my next visualization because it is an effective way to depict change over time. The visualization shows the 2 most populous countries from each continent with color repetition to categorize the lines by continent. This was done to help identify trends in population growth both over time and by continent. Two hover tooltips have been implemented to improve user interpretation.

  • The first hover tooltip can be done over the dots to present the particular population and other data at each highlighted year milestone.
  • The second hover tooltip can be done over the lines to gray out the rest of the countries not selected.

Bubble Chart

The bubble chart depicts how densely populated countries are in relation to their geographic size. The chart shows a country’s geographic size in relation to population density by location in relation to the x and y axis. The bubble size show's what percentage of the overall world's population percentage it makes up. This provides a better understanding of each country's impact relative to the space they take up. The same consistent color scale is used to categorize by continent labeled by the legend on the right.

  • Hover interaction over the bubbles has been added to specify more information for each country.

Bar Chart Race

My last visualization is a bar race. A Bar Chart Race was chosen to best visualize how the countries rank in terms of population size over a 50+ year period. Once again color is by continent following the color scheme consistent with the overall website in order to observe trends among countries by continent. Two races have been implemented, to show the leaders of each continent and how they rank in comparison to their counterparts.

  • The first race focuses on the top 5 most populous countries
  • The second shows the 2 most populous countries from each continent.
